Welcome!

Welcome to Just about anything you want to do with computers, where the title is pretty much self explanatory!

Here in our artblock we'll be doing a lot of stuff. Here are some options I've thought about.

  • Programming
  • Web Design
  • Graphical Design (2D)
  • Graphical Design (3D)
  • Fixing the 3D printer

Everything on this list is equally possible.

Structure

There are two ways this artblock will be structured. Here's one way...

  1. Arrive to the ArtBlock.

  2. Sit down at computer.

  3. If instructions page (like this one) is pulled up, read it at your own pace.

  4. Brennan or Richard adresses group on basics or unprovided information.

  5. Ask questions (if needed)

  6. Do work outlined by Brennan/Richard/Instructions

And here's the other...

  1. Arrive to the ArtBlock

  2. Sit down at computer.

  3. Do whatever you were doing last time for working on your final project until you're told to stop, or time is up, or you finish. If you weren't doing anything, ask Brennan / Richard for "final project" ideas.

  4. Listen to any further instructions if applicable.

Major Rules

  1. Absolutely no rough housing. Computers are FRAGILE, every part of them - from the monitor to the thing itself, heck, even the software.

  2. No bad language. If you can't say it with K-5th graders in the room, you can't say it here.

  3. BE ON TIME, OR HAVE A REAL EXCUSE! I expect you to show up ON TIME to this Artblock. If you're 5 minutes late.... oh well, I suppose. If you're any more than ~5 minutes late, I'll shake my fists. If you're more than 10 minutes late, you'd better have a teachers note, or have someone come see Richard or myself. There is no reason you should be late.

  4. Stay focused when working, and don't distract others. This means no misc. web browsing, no youtube videos, no funnies, etc. etc.

  5. No doing your homework or playing video games. This is an Artblock. Treat it like any other Artblock, not a study hall.

  6. Don't shout. The computer lab is small. We can hear you just fine.

  7. Try not to talk over others.

  8. Work only as partners if directed to. Most of the stuff we do is individual.

  9. Music is cool! This is one of the few "please do this" rules. Feel free to bring a device you can listen to music on if you have headphones. If everyone says OK, I'll bring a speaker and everyone can take turns playing a CLEAN playlist.

That just about sums it up.
